Characterizing Femininity

A Damsel in Distress

The Legend of Zelda A Link to the Past is an iconic game released in 1992. It starts with Link, the main character, hearing a voice in his head. This voice belongs to none other than Princess Zelda. The artifact chosen demonstrates this, with the Princess’s dialogue shown on screen while Link attempts to find her. She is seen saying “Help me… I am in the dungeon of the castle.” This artifact was chosen to demonstrate an incredibly common trope in female game characters: the damsel in distress. The damsel in distress plays the role of being weak, pitiable, and most importantly, trapped. Her character revolves around her needing saving. Curtis states that her role in the game “serves as the motivation for the protagonist, Link, to embark on his adventures” (2015). In a game focused towards young boys, a helpless princess in need of saving is the perfect kickstart into the game.

The damsel in distress also serves as another purpose: to be the prize for the player. By finding and saving Princess Zelda, the player can hope to discover more about her and connect Zelda to Link. Curtis argues that using Zelda as a reward system encourages the “disempowerment of the main female character” (2015). By being used as a prize for the character, Zelda is immediately labeled as weak.
