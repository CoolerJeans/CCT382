Characterizing Femininity

How Far is Too Far?

A more extreme side of sexualizing women in the games industry takes form in the common video game trope of sexual violence. The advertisement shown depicts a very young and orphaned “hottie” who is being chased by a deformed man. Hoffin argues that the prevalence of violence in the gaming industry along with the commonplace of oversexualizing female characters created the inevitable outcome of sexual violence in games (Hoffin & Lee-Treweek, 2020). This shows how the games industry has normalized violence against women. In some cases, Hoffin claims that “choosing to avoid [violence against women is] difficult, if not albeit impossible within certain genres”(Hoffin & Lee-Treweek, 2020). The casual attitude towards sexual violence is widely acceptable and commonplace, such that it is nearly unavoidable within the games industry. An effect of this is an increase in female characters that can be sexually harassed within games.

Sexual violence was able to become popular because people would buy it. A study shows that “objectified women and aggressive [men] are commonly depicted in video game marketing (e.g., game boxes) to reinforce hypermasculinity existing in culture” (Burgess et al., 2007). The popularity of this theme resulted in more sexual and violent attitudes and expectations for female game characters.
