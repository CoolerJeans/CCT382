Characterizing Femininity

Getting more Sensual

Games that were created for an older audience veered away from the classic damsel in distress and found themselves at scantily dressed female characters. The chosen artifact to demonstrate this is a magazine advertisement featuring a bikini wearing female character from Dead or Alive Xtreme Beach Volleyball. Along with the provocative photo, the advertisement includes crude and suggestive language. The advertisement starts with “Keeping your eye on the ball has never been harder ever since these bikini-clad babes hit the beach”. The advertisement is heavily suggestive that the game includes a lot of fan service, that being more bikini wearing girls.

Despite being a game about volleyball, the feature is advertising the characters. Instead of mentioning the sport, the clip talks about “playing Barbies with these characters–mising and matching outfit selections”. Their audience is not interested in purchasing or playing a sports game, the audience is interested in dressing and undressing the female players in different bikinis. “Video game boxes with sexualized female characters have the highest sales for teen- and mature-rated video games” (Burgess et al., 2007). The industry had created female characters as a sexual object to the point where they are normalized and in a point of interest among customers.
